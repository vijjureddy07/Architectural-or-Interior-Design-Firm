Residential

2025 · Hudson Valley, New York

The Hudson Valley Residence — Rewilding a 1920s Estate

A Grade II listed farmhouse on 40 acres of woodland demanded a dual mandate: preserve the original stone fabric while creating a contemporary family home for the 21st century. We introduced a new east-facing pavilion in weathering steel and glass that dialogues with — rather than mimics — the original structure. Biophilic principles guided the interior, dissolving boundaries between the living spaces and the surrounding meadow.

Residential

2025 · Tribeca, New York

The Tribeca Penthouse — Where Art Meets Architecture

This 6,400 sq ft full-floor penthouse required us to work around an extraordinary private art collection. The spatial arrangement became curatorial — walls, lighting, and circulation were designed to frame artworks rather than occupy them. Materials were selected for visual quiet: fluted limestone, raw silk, aged brass, and hand-polished concrete floors that recede rather than compete.

Hospitality

2024 · Dubai, UAE

The Saffron Club — Hospitality With Cultural Depth

Commissioned by a private members' club in Dubai's DIFC, this 12,000 sq ft interior weaves traditional Arab geometric motifs with a contemporary material palette of blackened steel, Calacatta marble, and handwoven textiles sourced from Moroccan artisans. The brief demanded spaces that could host intimate meetings by day and evolve into vibrant social environments by evening — achieved through a sophisticated dimmable lighting architecture.

Commercial

2024 · Mayfair, London

Meridian Capital HQ — Where Finance Meets Fine Design

A 22,000 sq ft headquarters for a boutique investment firm occupying the full upper three floors of a Georgian townhouse conversion. The challenge: preserve listed period features — original plasterwork, shutters, fireplaces — while inserting a fully modern workplace. The result is a layered dialogue between eras, where a contemporary open trading floor sits beneath restored Victorian cornicing.
